Animals in Icelandic songs

Many traditional Icelandic songs feature local animals, hinting at their relationship with humans and the mythology around them.

This page covers a few that have left a mark on Icelandic culture.

Ravens in Icelandic folksongs

Ravens feature in Icelandic history since the very beginning. According to the Book of Settlement, one of Iceland's first settlers, a Norwegian man named Hrafna-Flóki, or Raven-Flóki, used the flying direction of ravens to deduce where he would find the land, leading him to Iceland.

Ravens feature in many traditional Icelandic songs, where there's often an air of mystery and menace around them.

A song about ravens

Krummi svaf í klettagjá

The title of the song translates as "A raven slept in a gorge". On a cold winter night. His frozen beak can be seen popping up from the ground before day breaks.

Another verse has a raven calling the others to feast on a dead animal he has found on a frozen field.
