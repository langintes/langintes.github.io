Animals in Icelandic songs

Many traditional Icelandic songs feature local animals, hinting at their relationship with humans and the mythology around them.

This page covers a few that have left a mark on Icelandic culture.

Raven Fox Horse

Horses in Icelandic folksongs

Until the 20th century, horses were the main means of travel in Iceland as in much of the world. People would go through dangerous routes, over mountain ranges and deserts on horseback.

The horse tends to have a heroic and positive image in Icelandic song.

A song about horseriding

Á Sprengisandi (lyrics by Grímur Thomsen, song by Sigvaldi Kaldalóns)

The title of the song refers to a vast desert in Iceland's highlands which used to be a popular route of travel on horseback.

The song's lyrics emphasize the harshness of the nature and the many dangers that lurk in the highlands, among them from mysetrious outlaws, arctic foxes and an elfen queen.
